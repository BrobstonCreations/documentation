# 2018-2020 Winnebago Revel Countertop

### Tools Needed:
- Adjustable wrench
- Drill
- Drill bits
- Drill Stop Bit Collar Set (https://www.amazon.com/dp/B073VS72R3)
- Impact driver with philips bit or Philips screw driver
- 90 degree impact tool
- Oscillating tool
- Sharpie

### Brackets and fasteners included in the kit:
- CNC Machined Countertop

## Installation:
1. Shut off the AC Breakers for the Cooktop and all outlets

2. Remove the fridge door top fastener.
![3-remove-fridge-door-top-fastener.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Kitchen-Galley-Microwave-Install-Instructions-images/3-remove-fridge-door-top-fastener.jpg)

3. Tilt the fridge door forward and lift it off the bottom hinge.
![4-begin-removing-fridge-door.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Kitchen-Galley-Microwave-Install-Instructions-images/4-begin-removing-fridge-door.jpg)
![5-remove-fridge-door.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Kitchen-Galley-Microwave-Install-Instructions-images/5-remove-fridge-door.jpg)

4. Remove the four caps and screws from the front left and right sides of the fridge.
![6-remove-fridge-caps-and-screws.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Kitchen-Galley-Microwave-Install-Instructions-images/6-remove-fridge-caps-and-screws.jpg)

5. Begin removing the fridge.
![7-begin-removing-fridge.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Kitchen-Galley-Microwave-Install-Instructions-images/7-begin-removing-fridge.jpg)

6. Disconnect the fridge wiring.
![8-begin-disconnecting-fridge-wiring.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Kitchen-Galley-Microwave-Install-Instructions-images/8-begin-disconnecting-fridge-wiring.jpg)
![9-remove-fridge-wiring.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Kitchen-Galley-Microwave-Install-Instructions-images/9-remove-fridge-wiring.jpg)

7. Remove Microwave (if installed).

8. Remove the induction cooktop. There are four screws between the original mounting bracket and the countertop and four screws between the mounting bracket and the cooktop.

9. Remove kitchen galley drawers. With the drawers pulled as far our as they will travel, there is a black tab, one is pressed up, one is pressed down, and then the drawer will pull out. The middle drawer is a bit tricky, you'll have to push the slides in rather than pulling the drawer further out.

10. Remove the sink. Lift the glass top, there are three screws under ruber bumpers, loosen these. Disconnect the hot and cold water feeds for the faucet and disconnect the sink drain.

11. Remove all screws between the countertop and kitchen galley on either the countertop side or galley side of the bracket, whichever is easiest to remove.

12. Remove old countertop

13. Remove the vanity plate and unscrew the 110v outlet from the front left of the kitchen galley, a spacer will be installed (similar to the one below the bed on the other side of the bathroom).

14. Without installing any components in it, set the new kitchen galley countertop on top of the kitchen galley.

15. Trim the front support inside the kitchen galley to allow space for the sink. There may be a screw in the area that you are trimming, but sure to remove it before trimming. After you've finished trimming, (and be sure your screw is not too long, you may need to replace it with one shorter) drill a pilot hole with a drill bit stop to limit the depth, and replace the screw.
![PXL_20260227_150544943.MP.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260227_150544943.MP.jpg)
![PXL_20260227_150628333.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260227_150628333.jpg)

Set the sink into the countertop.
Swap the narrow mount in the back right (near the cooktop) with the wide one from the front left of the kitchen galley. Move the narrow mount into the furthest front left corner, verify with the countertop sitting on top and the sink sitting in it, that the bracket will not interfere. Screw the bracket into the kitchen galley.
Insert the sink faucet (and drinking water faucet of applicable) into the countertop holes. Thread the faucet nut on the bottom but so not install the rest of the faucet components.
With the countertop square to the pantry wall, as far forward as possible (after trimming the support), verify overall fitment and trim more of the support of necessary. If you are satisfied with the fitment, using a sharpie or other writing utensil, and begin marking the slots of the countertop mounts between the bracket and the countertop. I personally marked two, flipped the countertop, drilled a pilot hole, flipped the countertop back, and installed the two screws, then did two more, and repeated until finish. Use your best judgement here. I would recommend drill center of the slot as wood will expand and contract some as temperatures rise and fall.
Though I have not done this yet, I would recommend leaving a small gap and/or using a some silicone between the countertop and pantry wall. My setup developed a squeak after installing the new countertop right to the pantry wall.
