# 2018-2020 Winnebago Revel Countertop

### Tools Needed:
- Adjustable wrench
- Open ended wrenches
- Drill
- Drill bits
- Drill Stop Bit Collar Set (https://www.amazon.com/dp/B073VS72R3)
- Impact driver with philips bit or Philips screw driver
- 90 degree impact tool
- Oscillating tool
- Caulking Gun
- Sharpie
- Flash light of some sort
- Towel

### Consumables:
- Silicone Caulk
- Painters Tape
- Plumbers Putty

### Brackets and fasteners included in the kit:
- CNC Machined Countertop
- 120V Outlet Spacer

## Installation:
1. Shut off the AC Breakers for the Cooktop and all outlets

2. Remove the fridge door top fastener.
![3-remove-fridge-door-top-fastener.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Kitchen-Galley-Microwave-Install-Instructions-images/3-remove-fridge-door-top-fastener.jpg)

3. Tilt the fridge door forward and lift it off the bottom hinge.
![4-begin-removing-fridge-door.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Kitchen-Galley-Microwave-Install-Instructions-images/4-begin-removing-fridge-door.jpg)
![5-remove-fridge-door.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Kitchen-Galley-Microwave-Install-Instructions-images/5-remove-fridge-door.jpg)

4. Remove the four caps and screws from the front left and right sides of the fridge.
![6-remove-fridge-caps-and-screws.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Kitchen-Galley-Microwave-Install-Instructions-images/6-remove-fridge-caps-and-screws.jpg)

5. Begin removing the fridge.
![7-begin-removing-fridge.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Kitchen-Galley-Microwave-Install-Instructions-images/7-begin-removing-fridge.jpg)

6. Disconnect the fridge wiring.
![8-begin-disconnecting-fridge-wiring.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Kitchen-Galley-Microwave-Install-Instructions-images/8-begin-disconnecting-fridge-wiring.jpg)
![9-remove-fridge-wiring.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Kitchen-Galley-Microwave-Install-Instructions-images/9-remove-fridge-wiring.jpg)

7. Remove Microwave (if installed).

8. Remove the induction cooktop. There are four screws between the original mounting bracket and the countertop and four screws between the mounting bracket and the cooktop.

9. Remove kitchen galley drawers. With the drawers pulled as far our as they will travel, there is a black tab, one is pressed up, one is pressed down, and then the drawer will pull out. The middle drawer is a bit tricky, you'll have to push the slides in rather than pulling the drawer further out.

10. Remove the sink. Lift the glass top, there are three screws under ruber bumpers, loosen these. Disconnect the hot and cold water feeds for the faucet and disconnect the sink drain.

11. Remove all screws between the countertop and kitchen galley on either the countertop side or galley side of the bracket, whichever is easiest to remove.

12. Remove old countertop.

13. Swap the narrow mount in the back right (near the cooktop) with the wide one from the front left of the kitchen galley. Move the narrow mount into the furthest front left corner. Screw the bracket into the kitchen galley.
![PXL_20260227_150836670.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260227_150836670.jpg)

14. Because of the size and position of the sink, faucets, and slight change in induction cooktop position, you may need to move some of the original brackets holding the countertop to the kitchen galley. Here is a picture of where I positioned mine; your mileage may vary.
![PXL_20260305_194508930.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260305_194508930.jpg)

15. Remove the vanity plate and unscrew the 120v outlet from the front left of the kitchen galley, a spacer will be installed (similar to the one below the bed on the other side of the bathroom).

16. Without installing any components in it, set the new kitchen galley countertop on top of the kitchen galley.

17. Trim the front support inside the kitchen galley to allow space for the sink. There may be a screw in the area that you are trimming, but sure to remove it before trimming. After you've finished trimming, (and be sure your screw is not too long, you may need to replace it with one shorter) be sure to drill a pilot hole with a drill bit stop to limit the depth, and replace the screw. I would recommend marking a line and trim some of the support away with an oscillating tool as I cut away more than necessary.
![PXL_20260227_150544943.MP.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260227_150544943.MP.jpg)
![PXL_20260227_150628333.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260227_150628333.jpg)

18. Test fit the sink in the countertop (don't install the mounting hardware yet).

19. Insert the sink faucet (and drinking water faucet if applicable) into the countertop holes. Thread the faucet nut on the bottom but do not install the rest of the faucet components.

20. With the countertop square to the pantry wall, as far forward as possible (after trimming the support), verify overall fitment and trim more of the support if necessary. If you are satisfied with the fitment, using a sharpie or other writing utensil, begin marking the slots of the countertop mounts between the bracket and the countertop.
![PXL_20260227_145300377.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260227_145300377.jpg)

21. Remove the faucets and sink.
22. I would recommend drilling in the center of the slot that you marked as the countertop will expand and contract some as temperatures rise and fall. Be sure to use a drill bit stop collar and then drill pilot holes into the bottom of the countertop, triple check that you will not drill all the way through the countertop!
![PXL_20260227_145321128.MP.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260227_145321128.MP.jpg)
![PXL_20260227_145801009.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260227_145801009.jpg)

23. We are going to apply silicone between the countertop and the pantry wall, this should help prevent some sqeaking while driving and also create a seal between the countertop and pantry wall. Use painters tape to tape off the area to help contain the squeeze-out of silicone that will occur when we install the countertop.
![PXL_20260305_192607940.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260305_192607940.jpg)
![PXL_20260305_192938975.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260305_192938975.jpg)

24. Pull the countertop away from the wall and apply silicon to the wall. Press the countertop against the wall. I would wait until you have screwed down the countertop to remove the painters tape.
![PXL_20260305_193516721.MP.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260305_193516721.MP.jpg)

25. Install all the screws to mount the countertop to the kitchen galley and then remove the painters tape.
![PXL_20260227_150507238.MP.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260227_150507238.MP.jpg)

26. Use these next few steps as a test install before we apply silicone. Note: the silicone we will install is enough to hold the sink in place, for safe measure you can install the mounting hardware. For reference, while prototyping I made a second countertop and when uninstalling the sink I had to set the countertop/sink upside down on some 2x4's and sit on the bottom of the sink to get the silicone to release!

27. Install the sink mounting hardware. With the orange mounting pieces not folded over, insert the orange piece into the opening in the sink and then fold it over and it should click together. On only the left side, loosely install the metal bracket and screw as pictured. If this explanatin is confusing, refer to the sink installation instructions, it may describe this better than I have.
![PXL_20260227_152226215.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260227_152226215.jpg)

28. With only the metal bracket and screw installed on the left side, angle the sink in as pictured.
![PXL_20260227_152345228.MP.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260227_152345228.MP.jpg)
![PXL_20260227_152359900~2.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260227_152359900~2.jpg)
![PXL_20260227_152427797.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260227_152427797.jpg)

29. Uninstall the sink.

30. We need to apply silicone between the sink and the countertop to prevent water from getting below the sink. Tape off the countertop side of the sink and (while not pictured) tape off the sink side also.
![PXL_20260305_195022396.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260305_195022396.jpg)

31. Apply silicone.
![PXL_20260305_195506199.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260305_195506199.jpg)

32. Install sink
![PXL_20260305_200226072.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260305_200226072.jpg)

33. Install the right side orange sink mounting hardware.
![PXL_20260227_152526302.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260227_152526302.jpg)

34. Be sure not to over tighten! Using a long extension, tighten all four screws for the sink mounting hardware.
![PXL_20260227_152601787.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260227_152601787.jpg)

35. If not already installed, install the sink drain. I would recommend using plumbers putty.

36. Note: The last step of the drain install should be connecting the drain on the sink side, it is possible to accidently push up at the macerator end and break the 90 degree connector between the sink drain and the p-trap. If this happens you will have to order a replacement p-trap (available on Amazon) which is the only place I've been able to find that 90 degree piece.

37. Install the sink drain tube without the p-trap.
![PXL_20260305_200337847.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260305_200337847.jpg)

38. Tighten the worm clamps on the coupler above the macerator.
![PXL_20260305_201548093.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260305_201548093.jpg)

39. Attach the p-trap to the drain.
![PXL_20260305_200417173.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260305_200417173.jpg)

40. Be sure the black seal is inside the 90 degree drain connector. Thread tape should not be necessary here. If you have trouble getting this to seal, it may help.
![PXL_20260305_201256475.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260305_201256475.jpg)
![PXL_20260305_201330760.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260305_201330760.jpg)
![PXL_20260305_201356345.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260305_201356345.jpg)

41. Install the spacer for your 120V outlet as pictured.
![PXL_20260305_203046602.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260305_203046602.jpg)
![PXL_20260305_203103438.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260305_203103438.jpg)
![PXL_20260305_203113798.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260305_203113798.jpg)
![PXL_20260305_203221560.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260305_203221560.jpg)
![PXL_20260305_203228431.jpg](2018-2020-Winnebago-Revel-Countertop-Install-images/PXL_20260305_203228431.jpg)

42. Connect the hot and cold sides of your sink faucet. 
- Stock Plumbing: For the stock plumbing faucet lines are a bit long, you may need to coil them up and use a clamp to screw them to the inside of the kitchen galley, I would recommend doing this to the right of where the weight of the faucet moves up in down, you don't want that weight to have any obstructions or it may be annoying to use the faucet later. 
- RoamRig Plumbing: You will need two 12 inch faucet line extensions and one-way valves. If you have a filtered water solution you will also need a t-valve and I would recommend one with a quarter turn shut-off. This t-value can be before the one-way values.

43. Check for leaks on the pressure lines. I would recommend putting a towel below the sink area. With water in the fresh tank, turn on your water pump, using a flashlight check for leaks on any of the pressure side line connection points. If there are any leaks, tighten the connection point that is leaking; these should have a rubber seal inside them, if you find yourself needing to tighten them excessively, it is possible the rubber seal has shifted and you may need to disassemble, inspect, and realign the seal.

44. Check for leaks on the drain tube. Put enough water into the sink so that all connection points of the drain tube is coverd in water. Using a flashlight check for leaks at all the connection points. If there are any leaks, tighten the connection, these also should not need to be excessively tight, if they are and there is still a leak, I would recommend that you disassemble, inspect, and realign the seal. Worst case scenario, some thread tape on the threads at these connection points may help seal a small leak, though I have found that any connection with a rubber seal can be leak free if tightened the right amount, but not overtightened; tight enough to compress the rubber seal, but not flatten it.

45. Install your induction cooktop. Reinstall the rubber feet on the bottom of the cooktop using the machine screws that were removed when uninstalling the cooktop bracket. In our experience and with the intention to be able to optionally remove the cooktop to cook outside, the mounting hardware is not necessary. The cooktop should have some adhesive backed padding on the underside of the top lip which will help to prevent rattles.

46. Reinstall the microwave (if previously installed)
47. Reinstall the fridge.

- Notching the sink
- Check for leaks on both the pressured line connections and the drain with some water in the sink.
- If your top drawer interferes with the sink drain, it may need shortened or trimmed slightly.
